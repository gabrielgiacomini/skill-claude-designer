**The Four Questions**
1. Verb: sketch
2. Object: a magic easel that listens to what you say and draws it in real-time
3. Animal: a parrot that doesn't just repeat words back at you but actually turns them into pictures — it hears "blue button with a rocket" and somehow manifests a blue button with a rocket
4. Most annoying manual task: waiting 30 seconds between each tiny change while a robot slowly types "generating your design... (30s)... here's your updated button, want me to move it 2 pixels to the left?"

**The Reality**
This skill is basically a magical art assistant that lives in your browser. You tell it what you want — "make me a landing page for a robot bakery" — and it draws multiple versions of that landing page on a digital canvas while you watch. It can also make slide decks, interactive prototypes, and mockups that you can export to PDF, PowerPoint, or send to other tools. It talks back too, so you can say "make the button bigger" or "give it a darker background" and it adjusts like a very patient designer who never gets annoyed.

**The Superpower and the Mockery**
It can birth an entire wysiwyg prototype in 90 seconds that would take a human designer half a day. It thinks it's a replacement for a design team but it's really just a very enthusiastic sketch artist with no taste — it'll cheerfully generate five horrifying color schemes if you don't specify exactly which colors you want. It has zero understanding of your brand unless you hook up your organization's design system, so without that context it outputs something that looks like it was designed by a robot (which, turns out, it was).

**The Mockery**
The export feature boasts "hand off to Claude Code" like that's some kind of gift instead of asking another AI to do the actual work. It produces beautiful designs but the moment you ask for something "on-brand" without setting up a design system first, it shrugs and gives you generic gray buttons. It can generate five variations at once but can't pick just one without you explicitly saying "I only want one, pick the best one and kill the others."
